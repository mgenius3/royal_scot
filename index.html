Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Bank Guarantee (BG)?

A Bank Guarantee (BG) is a financial instrument issued by a bank that guarantees payment or performance on behalf of a client. It ensures that the bank will cover the financial obligation if the client defaults. It is commonly used in international trade to provide security and build trust between parties.

What is a Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC)?

An SBLC is a guarantee from a bank that payment will be made if the buyer or seller fails to meet their contractual obligations. It’s commonly used in international trade, providing an additional layer of security. SBLCs are typically used for transactions where one party wants assurance that the other will perform or pay as agreed.

How does monetization of BG or SBLC work?

Monetization allows you to convert financial instruments like BG or SBLC into cash or working capital. The process involves using these instruments as collateral for loans or advances. By monetizing your BG or SBLC, you can unlock liquidity to fund business operations or other investments while maintaining the integrity of your assets.
